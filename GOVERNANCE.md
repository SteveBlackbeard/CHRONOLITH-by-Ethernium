# CONTINUITY LEGACY: Project Governance & Resilience 🏛️🧬

This document defines how **CONTINUITY LEGACY** is maintained and how its longevity is ensured beyond a single maintainer.

## 1. The Continuity Principle (Project Longevity)
The core risk of a "1-maintainer" project is the loss of strategic context. CONTINUITY LEGACY mitigates this by applying its own framework to its own development:
- **Canonical Logs**: Every architectural decision is recorded in `.continuity/DECISIONS_LOG.md`.
- **State Manifests**: Every project phase is codified in `STATE.json`.
- **Succession**: In the event of maintainer inactivity, any contributor can use the existing continuity files as a "Lifeboat" to resume development with 100% contextual fidelity.

## 2. Decision Making
- **Strategic Direction**: Managed by the Project Lead (current: SteveBlackbeard).
- **Technical Proposals**: Evaluated via the **Decision Engine** (Pro version) to ensure alignment with the "Universal Core" principles.
- **Strict Mode**: No contribution will be accepted unless it passes the `--strict` validation cycle, ensuring the quality remains industrial-grade.

## 3. Contributing
We welcome developers who value technical excellence and contextual continuity:
- **Lite Contributions**: Focused on performance and zero-dependency core logic.
- **Pro Contributions**: Focused on tactical automation and user-driven features.
- **Omega Contributions**: Focused on AI/ML integration and cognitive mapping.

## 4. Maintenance Health
To reduce "Bus Factor" risk, we aim to:
1.  **Automate Validation**: Shift the burden of code quality to the pre-push hooks and GitHub Actions.
2.  **Transparent Evolution**: Maintain a clear `ROADMAP.md` that defines the future of all three editions.
